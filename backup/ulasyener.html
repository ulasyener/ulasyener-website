About · Bio
Ulas Yener is an interdisciplinary artistic researcher, architect, and media practitioner currently pursuing his Ph.D. at the Faculty of Art and Design at Bauhaus-Universitat Weimar. He completed his M.Sc. in MediaArchitecture at Bauhaus-Universitat Weimar in 2021 and received his Bachelor degree in Interior Architecture and Environmental Design from the Faculty of Fine Arts at Hacettepe University in 2012.

His research and artistic practice focus on migration, media archaeology, archives, memory, and audiovisual storytelling. For several years, he has been developing Fremde Turen - El Kapilari, an autoethnographic media archive examining the cultural history and media representations of Turkish immigration in Germany through cinema, music, literature, oral histories, and archival materials.

Working across essayistic documentary, archival research, sound, and experimental media practices, his projects investigate the relationships between personal memory, collective history, and cultural production.
